## 9. Abstract Strategy for This Audience

**The q-bio reader is a physicist or mathematician who does biology.** Comfortable with differential equations, FBA, stochastic processes, rate constants. Skeptical of hand-wavy biological claims without quantitative support.

**What will land:**
- "We model post-extraction ATP synthesis decay as a time-dependent process using pFBA with protein-half-life-constrained bounds"
- "The transit window is defined as the time to reach minimum ATP flux below the mitophagy activation threshold"
- "We predict transit window = N hours under arterial blood substrate concentrations"

**What will not land:**
- "Mitochondrial transplantation shows clinical promise"
- "This could help patients with ischemia"

**Title adjustment for this audience:**

Current: *"Predicting the Functional Transit Window for Extracellular Mitochondria Using Genome-Scale Metabolic Modeling and Protein Turnover Kinetics"*

Better: *"Time-Dependent ATP Flux Decay in Extracted Mammalian Mitochondria: A Genome-Scale Metabolic Model with Protein Turnover Kinetics"*

Lead with the computational method. "Functional transit window" sounds clinical. q-bio readers want to see the mathematical structure in the title.
